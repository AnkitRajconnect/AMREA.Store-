Oil, not alcohol

Not a perfume.
A layer of skin.

Alcohol lifts a fragrance off the skin and releases it into the room. It announces. It performs. Then it evaporates within the hour, leaving nothing behind.

Oil is different. It absorbs warmth. It settles into the specific temperature of your body, the pace of your day, the quiet of your afternoon. It moves with you rather than away from you.

Six hours after application, something remains — not a trace of the original scent, but a new thing. Something that belongs to you, and only to you.
