Composition
Opening — first hour

Cedar, resin, warm wood

On first contact: a quiet density. The cedar is not forest — it is the interior of an old chest. The resin sits underneath, slow and dry. There is no sharpness.

Heart — hours two through four

Amber, skin, the warm note

The fragrance settles. The amber is not sweet — it is geological, the warmth of something pressed and aged for a long time. It begins to absorb the temperature of the skin.

Drydown — the hours that remain

Patchouli, musk, time

This is where it becomes personal. The patchouli is earthy without being heavy. The musk is almost invisible. What remains is not the fragrance — it is you, wearing it.

Made for skin,
not for the room.

Most fragrances are designed to project. They are made to cross a space, to arrive before you do, to make an impression from several feet away. Alcohol makes this possible — it is a vehicle for distance.

The Warm Note does not project. It holds. Applied to the wrist, the crook of the elbow, the side of the neck — it stays close. For the first hour it is almost imperceptible to the wearer. The oil is absorbing warmth, adjusting.

The second hour is different. Something begins to emerge — not a perfume, but a quality. Warm. Slightly woody. Resinous in a way that does not feel heavy. Skin-close and slow.

By the fourth hour it is entirely your own. The base chemistry of the oil has responded to your specific temperature, your movement through the day, the particular warmth of your skin. Someone beside you will notice something they cannot categorise — something quiet and present and distinctly yours.

This is what oil does that alcohol cannot. It does not evaporate. It stays. It becomes part of the surface it was placed on. It absorbs the day and returns it as something different.

Wear it on clean, lightly moisturised skin. Apply before you dress. Do not rub. Let it sit. The warm note will find you, in its own time.

The world it arrives in

Morning light through linen

The diffuse warmth before the day has begun. Slow, soft, with no urgency to it.

A worn leather notebook

Something that has been held often. That carries the warmth of previous afternoons in its surface.

Warm stone, late afternoon

The heat retained from a long day. Dry, mineral, faintly earthy.

A quiet room with dark wood

Unhurried. Dense without being heavy. Familiar without being common.

What it takes

The oil is blended in Grasse in a workshop that has changed very little in forty years. The formula was developed over several months, working with a perfumer who declined to use ingredients they couldn't source directly.

Each bottle is filled by hand. Each vial is dark glass, to protect the oil from light. There are no batch numbers. There is no automation.

  • Cold-pressed carrier oil base
  • Natural resin and cedar extraction
  • Small-batch hand-filling in Grasse
  • Dark glass vial — 15ml
  • No fixatives. No synthetics in the base.

The process above took eight months. The oil has been blended twice and adjusted once. The formula is now fixed. When these forty bottles are gone, the first collection ends.
